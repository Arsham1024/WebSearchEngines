          <h2>
           <span class="mw-headline" id="About_Wikipedia">
            About Wikipedia
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div id="mf-intro" title="Welcome!">
           This is the front page of the
           <b>
            <a href="/wiki/Simple_English_Wikipedia" title="Simple English Wikipedia">
             Simple English Wikipedia
            </a>
           </b>
           .
           <i>
            Wikipedias
           </i>
           are places where people work together to write
           <a href="/wiki/Encyclopedia" title="Encyclopedia">
            encyclopedias
           </a>
           in different languages. We use
           <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Simple_English_Wikipedia#Simple_English" title="Wikipedia:Simple English Wikipedia">
            Simple English
           </a>
           words and
           <a href="/wiki/Grammar" title="Grammar">
            grammar
           </a>
           here. The Simple English Wikipedia is for everyone! That includes children and adults who are learning English.
There are 196,214 articles on the Simple English Wikipedia. All of the pages are
           <a href="/wiki/Open_content" title="Open content">
            free to use
           </a>
           . They have all been published under both the
           <i>
            <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Text_of_Creative_Commons_Attribution-ShareAlike_3.0_Unported_License" title="Wikipedia:Text of Creative Commons Attribution-ShareAlike 3.0 Unported License">
             Creative Commons Attribution/Share-Alike License 3.0
            </a>
           </i>
           and the
           <i>
            <a href="/wiki/GNU_Free_Documentation_License" title="GNU Free Documentation License">
             GNU Free Documentation License
            </a>
           </i>
           .
           <b>
            You can help here!
           </b>
           You may change these pages and make new pages. Read the
           <a href="/wiki/Help:Contents" title="Help:Contents">
            help pages
           </a>
           and other
           <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Useful" title="Wikipedia:Useful">
            good pages
           </a>
           to learn how to
           <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Wikipedia:How_to_write_Simple_English_articles" title="Wikipedia:How to write Simple English articles">
            write pages here
           </a>
           . If you need help, you may ask questions at
           <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Simple_talk" title="Wikipedia:Simple talk">
            Simple talk
           </a>
           .
          </div>
          <hr style="width: 80%; margin: 1em 10%;"/>
          <p>
           When writing articles here:
          </p>
          <ul>
           <li>
            <b>
             Use
             <a href="/wiki/Basic_English" title="Basic English">
              Basic English
             </a>
             words and shorter sentences.
            </b>
            This allows people to understand complex terms or phrases.
           </li>
           <li>
            <b>
             Write good pages.
            </b>
            The best encyclopedia pages have useful, well-written information.
           </li>
           <li>
            <b>
             Use the pages to learn and teach.
            </b>
            These pages can help people learn English. You can also use them to
            <a class="extiw" href="https://meta.wikimedia.org/wiki/How_to_start_a_new_Wikipedia" title="meta:How to start a new Wikipedia">
             make a new Wikipedia
            </a>
            to help other people.
           </li>
           <li>
            <b>
             Simple does not mean short.
            </b>
            Writing in Simple English means that simple words are used. It does not mean readers want basic information. Articles do not have to be short to be simple; expand articles, add details, but use basic
            <a href="/wiki/Vocabulary" title="Vocabulary">
             vocabulary
            </a>
            .
           </li>
           <li>
            <b>
             Be bold!
            </b>
            Your article does not have to be perfect, because other editors will fix it and make it better. And most importantly, do not be afraid to start and make articles better yourself.
           </li>
          </ul>

           <span class="mw-headline" id="Selected_article">
            Selected article
           </span>
          </h2>
          <div class="floatleft">
           <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Goeringcaptivity2.jpg">
            <img alt="Goeringcaptivity2.jpg" data-file-height="272" data-file-width="206" decoding="async" height="169" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Goeringcaptivity2.jpg/128px-Goeringcaptivity2.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Goeringcaptivity2.jpg/192px-Goeringcaptivity2.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/5/51/Goeringcaptivity2.jpg 2x" width="128"/>
           </a>
          </div>
          <p>
           <b>
            <a href="/wiki/Hermann_G%C3%B6ring" title="Hermann Göring">
             Hermann Wilhelm Göring
            </a>
           </b>
           (12 January 1893 in
           <a href="/wiki/Rosenheim" title="Rosenheim">
            Rosenheim
           </a>
           ,
           <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Upper_Bavaria_Government_Region" title="Upper Bavaria Government Region">
            Upper Bavaria
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="/wiki/Bavaria" title="Bavaria">
            Bavaria
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">
            Germany
           </a>
           – 15 October 1946 in
           <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg" title="Nuremberg">
            Nuremberg
           </a>
           ,
           <a href="/wiki/Middle_Franconia" title="Middle Franconia">
            Middle Franconia
           </a>
           , Bavaria, Germany) was a
           <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">
            German
           </a>
           <a href="/wiki/Politician" title="Politician">
            politician
           </a>
           and
           <a href="/wiki/Military" title="Military">
            military
           </a>
           leader of Germany. Between 1932 and 1945, he was
           <a href="/wiki/President" title="President">
            President
           </a>
           of the German
           <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Reichstag" title="Reichstag">
            Reichstag
           </a>
           and also a
           <a href="/wiki/Minister_(government)" title="Minister (government)">
            minister
           </a>
           of the
           <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Third_Reich" title="Third Reich">
            Third Reich
           </a>
           . In the
           <a href="/wiki/World_War_II" title="World War II">
            Second World War
           </a>
           , he was the
           <a href="/wiki/Commander-in-Chief" title="Commander-in-Chief">
            Commander-in-Chief
           </a>
           of the
           <a href="/wiki/Luftwaffe" title="Luftwaffe">
            German air force
           </a>
           . Göring was responsible for the creation of the
           <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Concentration_camps" title="Concentration camps">
            concentration camps
           </a>
           and the
           <a href="/wiki/Gestapo" title="Gestapo">
            Gestapo
           </a>
           , the official secret
           <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Nazi" title="Nazi">
            Nazi
           </a>
           police. In 1941, he gave
           <a href="/wiki/Reinhard_Heydrich" title="Reinhard Heydrich">
            Reinhard Heydrich
           </a>
           the order to arrange the
           <a href="/wiki/Final_Solution" title="Final Solution">
            Final Solution
           </a>
           to kill millions of
           <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Jews" title="Jews">
            Jews
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
           Göring was one of the 24 people
           <a href="/wiki/Indictment" title="Indictment">
            charged
           </a>
           at the
           <a href="/wiki/Nuremberg_Trials" title="Nuremberg Trials">
            Nuremberg Trials
           </a>
           . The court
           <a href="/wiki/Verdict" title="Verdict">
            decided
           </a>
           he was guilty. The judgment on 1 October 1946 said that he was to be
           <a class="mw-disambig" href="/wiki/Hanging" title="Hanging">
            hanged
           </a>
           , but he
           <a href="/wiki/Suicide" title="Suicide">
            killed himself
           </a>
           by swallowing a tablet of
           <a href="/wiki/Cyanide" title="Cyanide">
            cyanide
           </a>
           before he could be
           <a href="/wiki/Execution" title="Execution">
            executed
           </a>
           .
          </p>
          <p>
