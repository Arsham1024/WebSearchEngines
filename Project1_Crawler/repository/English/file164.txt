      <div class="wp-block-shiro-landing-page-hero hero is-style-yellow50">
       <header class="hero__header">
        <div class="hero__text-column">
         <small class="hero__kicker">
          Technology
         </small>
         <h1 class="hero__title">
          We keep Wikimedia projects fast, reliable, and available to all.
         </h1>
        </div>
        <figure class="hero__image-container image-filter-inherit">
         <img alt="" class="hero__image" src="https://wikimediafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2018/07/participant_on_mobile_phone_wikidemocracy_event_may_2017_302-e1533253842592.jpg?w=1024&amp;h=675&amp;crop=1"/>
        </figure>
       </header>
       <div class="hero__intro">
        <p>
         From hosting Wikipedia to creating edit-checking artificial intelligence (AI), we design and build the open-source technology that powers Wikimedia projects. Community volunteers and Foundation technologists collaborate on MediaWiki, the platform that makes sharing free knowledge possible.
        </p>
       </div>
      </div>
      <div class="wp-block-shiro-stairs">
       <div class="wp-block-shiro-stair stair">
        <h2 class="stair__heading is-style-h3">
         Hosting the world’s knowledge
        </h2>
        <img class="wp-image-61217 size-image_16x9_small stair__image" height="338" loading="lazy" src="https://wikimediafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/Papaul_Tshibamba-4-e1565971446217.jpg?w=600&amp;h=338&amp;crop=1" width="600"/>
        <p class="stair__body">
         Wikipedia is the fifth most-visited website in the world, with more than 6,000 visits every second.
         <a href="/2018/01/09/technology-department-highlights/">
          Our infrastructure keeps it online
         </a>
         . Over the last year, we delivered an uptime of 99.97% (according to independent monitoring) across all project sites.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="wp-block-shiro-stair stair">
        <h2 class="stair__heading is-style-h3">
         Saving data costs for readers
        </h2>
        <img class="wp-image-61216 size-image_16x9_small stair__image" height="338" loading="lazy" sizes="(max-width: 600px) 100vw, 600px" src="https://wikimediafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/Page_previews_mockup.jpg?w=600&amp;h=338&amp;crop=1" srcset="https://wikimediafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/Page_previews_mockup.jpg 1024w, https://wikimediafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/Page_previews_mockup.jpg?resize=300,169 300w, https://wikimediafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/Page_previews_mockup.jpg?resize=768,432 768w, https://wikimediafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/Page_previews_mockup.jpg?resize=600,338 600w" width="600"/>
        <p class="stair__body">
         More than half of Wikimedia’s visitors connect from mobile devices. But high mobile data costs across Africa, Latin America, and Asia can make accessing Wikipedia expensive. That is why
         <a href="/2016/09/19/mobile-web-improvements/">
          we redesigned our apps and sites
         </a>
         to be up to 51% less data intensive.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="wp-block-shiro-stair stair">
        <h2 class="stair__heading is-style-h3">
         Assisting human collaboration with machine learning
        </h2>
        <img class="wp-image-61218 size-image_16x9_small stair__image" height="338" loading="lazy" src="https://wikimediafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/Revscore_WP-e1565971438347.jpg?w=600&amp;h=338&amp;crop=1" width="600"/>
        <p class="stair__body">
         Anyone can edit Wikipedia, and it can be challenging to maintain quality control. That is why we made
         <a href="/2015/11/30/artificial-intelligence-x-ray-specs/">
          ORES,
         </a>
         an artificial intelligence service that automatically identifies potentially problematic content as soon as it appears, advising editors if changes are needed.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="wp-block-shiro-stair stair">
        <h2 class="stair__heading is-style-h3">
         Helping NASA
        </h2>
        <img class="wp-image-61215 size-image_16x9_small stair__image" height="338" loading="lazy" src="https://wikimediafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/M101_hires_STScI-PRC2006-10a-e1565971453537.jpg?w=600&amp;h=338&amp;crop=1" width="600"/>
        <p class="stair__body">
         <a href="/2016/05/05/mediawiki-nasa/">
          NASA’s internal wiki
         </a>
         for collaboration and documentation is built on MediaWiki, just like Wikipedia. We create MediaWiki—open-source wiki software that is free and available for anyone.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="wp-block-shiro-stair stair">
        <h2 class="stair__heading is-style-h3">
         Mentoring new developers
        </h2>
        <img class="wp-image-61219 size-image_16x9_small stair__image" height="338" loading="lazy" src="https://wikimediafoundation.org/wp-content/uploads/2019/07/Wikimedia_Hackathon_2013_-_Flickr_-_Sebastiaan_ter_Burg_9-e1565971430820.jpg?w=600&amp;h=338&amp;crop=1" width="600"/>
        <p class="stair__body">
         To help build a more diverse and inclusive technical community, the Wikimedia Foundation
         <a href="/2017/12/01/six-outreachy-interns-funded/">
          funds, supports, and mentors new developers
         </a>
         and underrepresented groups in tech.
        </p>
       </div>
       <div class="wp-block-shiro-stair stair">
        <h2 class="stair__heading is-style-h3">
         Growing a volunteer developer community
        </h2>
