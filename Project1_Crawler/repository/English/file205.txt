        <tbody>
         <tr style="font-size: 1.8em; color:#333; background: #f4f4f4;">
          <td colspan="2">
           What are Wikimedia user groups?
          </td>
         </tr>
         <tr>
          <td style="width:65%;">
           <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" lang="en">
            <p>
             Wikimedia user groups are
             <a class="extiw" href="https://foundation.wikimedia.org/wiki/Vote:Movement_Roles_Letter,_February_2012" title="wmf:Vote:Movement Roles Letter, February 2012">
              intended to be simple and flexible affiliates
             </a>
             that are an alternative to
             <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia_chapters" title="Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia chapters">
              chapters
             </a>
             and
             <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia_thematic_organizations" title="Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia thematic organizations">
              thematic organizations
             </a>
             - which require more formal requirements. User groups are highly valued as equal players in the Wikimedia movement. They are meant to empower and engage people around the world to collect and develop educational content under a free license or in the public domain, and to disseminate it effectively and globally.
            </p>
            <p>
             Recognition from the
             <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Affiliations_Committee" title="Special:MyLanguage/Affiliations Committee">
              Affiliations Committee
             </a>
             allows a group to apply for using the
             <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia_trademarks" title="Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia trademarks">
              Wikimedia trademarks
             </a>
             and to get certain
             <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grants:Index" title="Grants:Index">
              grants
             </a>
             ; however, recognition is
             <b>
              not
             </b>
             required to do any of the work typical user groups do or might do, it is an optional extra step with connected benefits and some requirements.
            </p>
           </div>
          </td>
         </tr>

         <tr style="font-size: 1.8em; color:#333; background: #f4f4f4;">
          <td colspan="2">
           Why should we apply?
          </td>
         </tr>
         <tr>
          <td style="width:65%;">
           <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" lang="en">
            <p>
             Recognition from the
             <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Affiliations_Committee" title="Special:MyLanguage/Affiliations Committee">
              Affiliations Committee
             </a>
             allows a group to apply for using the
             <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia_trademarks" title="Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia trademarks">
              Wikimedia trademarks
             </a>
             and to get certain
             <a class="mw-redirect" href="/wiki/Grants:Index" title="Grants:Index">
              grants
             </a>
             ; however, recognition is
             <b>
              not
             </b>
             required to do any of the work typical user groups do or might do, it is an optional extra step with connected benefits and some requirements.
            </p>
            <p>
             Benefits of becoming a recognized Wikimedia user group includes:
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              Use the Wikimedia logos consistent with the Wikimedia Foundation's
              <a class="extiw" href="https://foundation.wikimedia.org/wiki/Trademark_policy" title="wmf:Trademark policy">
               Trademark Policy
              </a>
              without any additional approval. User groups may use other logos not part of the Trademark Policy (such as the Wikimedia Foundation logo) pursuant to a trademark license with the Wikimedia Foundation and approval by the Wikimedia Foundation legal team.
             </li>
             <li>
              Receive large, but limited, quantities of Wikimedia merchandise for use in activities and outreach efforts.
             </li>
             <li>
              Public recognition of the group's affiliation with the Wikimedia Foundation.
             </li>
             <li>
              Affiliations Committee Liaison support during and after the approval process.
             </li>
             <li>
              One scholarship to attend the
              <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia_Conference" title="Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia Conference">
               Wikimedia Conference
              </a>
              , held annually in Berlin for Wikimedia movement affiliates.
             </li>
            </ul>
            <p>
             Without recognition, groups may still:
            </p>
            <ul>
             <li>
              Engage in group activities to promote, build, and support the Wikimedia movement and its projects.
             </li>
             <li>
              Utilize the Wikimedia Community logo.
             </li>
             <li>
              Request one-time use of Wikimedia logos consistent with the Wikimedia Foundation's
              <a class="extiw" href="https://foundation.wikimedia.org/wiki/Trademark_policy" title="wmf:Trademark policy">
               Trademark Policy
              </a>
              pursuant to a trademark license with the Wikimedia Foundation and approval by the Wikimedia Foundation legal team.
             </li>
             <li>
              Apply for
              <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Grants:PEG" title="Special:MyLanguage/Grants:PEG">
               Project &amp; Event Grants
              </a>
              ,
              <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Grants:IEG" title="Special:MyLanguage/Grants:IEG">
               Individual Engagement Grants
              </a>
              , and
              <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Grants:TPS" title="Special:MyLanguage/Grants:TPS">
               Travel &amp; Participation Support
              </a>
              grants.
             </li>
            </ul>
           </div>
          </td>
         </tr>
        </tbody>
       </table>
       <p>
        <ins id="eligibility">
        </ins>
       </p>
       <div style="background: #f4f4f4; padding: 1em;">
        <div style="font-size: 1.8em; color:#333;">
         What are the eligibility requirements?
        </div>
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Main page:
           <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia_user_groups/Requirements" title="Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia user groups/Requirements">
            Wikimedia user groups/Requirements
           </a>
           <span style="display:none">
            <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_user_groups/Requirements" title="Wikimedia user groups/Requirements">
            </a>
           </span>
          </i>
         </dd>
        </dl>
        <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" lang="en">
         <p>
          <b>
           Wikimedia user groups
          </b>
          are
          <a class="extiw" href="https://foundation.wikimedia.org/wiki/Vote:Movement_Roles_Letter,_February_2012" title="wmf:Vote:Movement Roles Letter, February 2012">
           intended to be simple and flexible affiliates
          </a>
          with far fewer requirements than
          <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia_chapters" title="Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia chapters">
           chapters
          </a>
          and
          <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia_thematic_organizations" title="Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia thematic organizations">
           thematic organizations
          </a>
          . Even affiliates hoping to become a chapter or thematic organization are not expected to meet the requirements for those affiliate models to apply for Wikimedia user group recognition.
         </p>
        </div>
        <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" lang="en">
         <p>
          The requirements to set up an officially recognized Wikimedia user group are meant to be light-weight and easy to follow.
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           <b>
            Three active Wikimedia editors
           </b>
           <br/>
           At least 3 members with 300 or more contributions to a Wikimedia project on a registered account that has existed for more than 6 months in good community standing (meaning they are not currently suspended or otherwise prevented from participating). While user groups should welcome the input of people who are not active contributors to the Wikimedia projects, and are required to allow new members to join, they should not stray too far from the community. An active involvement of contributors to the Wikimedia projects is necessary to enable a user group to bring its real-life initiatives to life in the Wikimedia projects.
          </li>
          <li>
           <b>
            Agree to Wikimedia user group agreement and code of conduct
           </b>
           <br/>
           Your group must agree to the
           <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia_user_groups/Agreement_and_code_of_conduct" title="Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia user groups/Agreement and code of conduct">
            Wikimedia user group agreement and code of conduct
           </a>
           - which outlines basic expectations of Wikimedia user groups, such as on-wiki documentation of activities, operating within
           <a class="extiw" href="https://foundation.wikimedia.org/wiki/Resolution:Wikimedia_Foundation_Guiding_Principles" title="wmf:Resolution:Wikimedia Foundation Guiding Principles">
            Wikimedia guiding principles
           </a>
           , and compliance with the
           <a class="extiw" href="https://foundation.wikimedia.org/wiki/Trademark_policy" title="wmf:Trademark policy">
            Trademark policy
           </a>
           . When you submit your application, you will be asked to agree to both the Wikimedia user group agreement and code of conduct.
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          If your user group application is not able to meet these criteria you are still welcome to apply but we would like to have a small motivation on why you think these criteria are not important to your initiative. Please address these two questions in your motivation
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           How would your user group contribute to the mission of the Wikimedia Movement
          </li>
          <li>
           How does the background of the founding members contribute to the success of your user group
          </li>
         </ol>
         <p>
          Examples of applications that could have good reasons for not meeting the criteria are things like research groups or other initiatives which do not require you to be an active contributor.
         </p>
         <p>
          Please be aware that if you do not meet the three criteria outlined above, the Affiliations Committee will need more time to discuss your application (and hopefully approve it), this might also involve some more communication with the founding members of the user group application, so please watch your inbox for mails on this topic so that you can respond to them quickly.
         </p>
        </div>
       </div>
       <p>
        <ins id="applying">
        </ins>
       </p>
       <div style="background: #f4f4f4; padding: 1em;">
        <div style="font-size: 1.8em; color:#333;">
         How do we apply for recognition?
        </div>
        <dl>
         <dd>
          <i>
           Main page:
           <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia_user_groups/Creation_guide" title="Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia user groups/Creation guide">
            Wikimedia user groups/Creation guide
           </a>
           <span style="display:none">
            <a href="/wiki/Wikimedia_user_groups/Creation_guide" title="Wikimedia user groups/Creation guide">
            </a>
           </span>
          </i>
         </dd>
        </dl>
        <div class="mw-content-ltr" dir="ltr" lang="en">
         <p>
          Once you've gathered the group, the wiki page, and the big ideas on what you want to do, visit the
          <a href="/wiki/Special:Contact/affcomusergroup" title="Special:Contact/affcomusergroup">
           Wikimedia user group application page
          </a>
          and apply for recognition. The Affiliations Committee may ask you some questions, review your application, and if appropriate will submit them to the Wikimedia Foundation to recognize your group as an official Wikimedia User Group.  Initial recognition is normally for one year, and is then reviewed.  Recognition can be suspended or removed if necessary if a group becomes inactive.
         </p>
         <p>
          Delays during the application approval process most often arise from incomplete applications or applications that need to be modified before they can be approved. To prevent this from happening and help expedite your group's approval process, please double check this list before submitting your application:
         </p>
         <ol>
          <li>
           Does your name meet the
           <a href="/wiki/Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia_movement_affiliates_naming_guidelines" title="Special:MyLanguage/Wikimedia movement affiliates naming guidelines">
            affiliates naming requirements
           </a>
           ? Group discussions amongst Wikimedia user groups regarding their name can take time, and generally you want to avoid having to re-engage in that conversation after your application has been submitted. You may contact the Affiliations Committee to seek input on your name before submitting your application.
          </li>
          <li>
           Does your group have a clear scope and purpose that is consistently and clearly explained? If your Meta page explains your user group in a way that differs from your application, and if those differ from the activities your group is undertaking - that may delay the application's approval as a clear scope and purpose is required for the resolution to approve the user group and the user group agreement which the two primary contacts will be signing with the Wikimedia Foundation. Your scope and purpose statement should clearly and concisely explain what your group hopes to accomplish and who would be interested in joining. We recognize this may evolve over time, but you should have a clear commitment to a specific statement at the time of your application.
          </li>
          <li>
           Are your two primary contacts willing to identify themselves with the Wikimedia Foundation? Sometimes privacy concerns might cause someone to change their minds, which can delay the final steps in the user group recognition process.
          </li>
          <li>
           Is your group ready to accept new members and already include at least three Wikimedia editors in good standing? Groups which have a closed or limited membership model may require more discussion and review. Groups which do not have at least three active members who are also active Wikimedia editors in good standing will not be approved.
          </li>
          <li>
           Are your designated leaders and group as a whole willing to follow the Wikimedia user group agreement and code of conduct? All leaders who are named on the application must agree prior to the application being sent. Applicants found to be violating the agreement or code of conduct will not be approved, and existing Wikimedia user groups found to be violating the agreement or code of conduct may have their affiliation status rescinded.
          </li>
         </ol>
        </div>
