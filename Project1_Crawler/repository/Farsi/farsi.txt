           <p>
            The
            <b>
             <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_blackcap" title="Eurasian blackcap">
              Eurasian blackcap
             </a>
            </b>
            (
            <i>
             Sylvia atricapilla
            </i>
            ) is a
            <a href="/wiki/Typical_warbler" title="Typical warbler">
             typical warbler
            </a>
            in Europe, western Asia and northern  Africa. It has grey upperparts and paler grey underparts, and the male has a black cap, while the female's is reddish-brown. The male's song is a rich musical warbling. The blackcap builds a neat cup nest in scrub, often in mature
            <a href="/wiki/Deciduous" title="Deciduous">
             deciduous
            </a>
            woodland. The female lays four to six
            <a href="/wiki/Buff_(colour)" title="Buff (colour)">
             buff
            </a>
            eggs, which hatch in about 11 days. The chicks
            <a href="/wiki/Fledge" title="Fledge">
             fledge
            </a>
            in 11–12 days, but are fed by both adults for some time after. Birds from the colder areas of the range
            <a href="/wiki/Bird_migration" title="Bird migration">
             migrate
            </a>
            , wintering in scrub in western Europe, around the Mediterranean and in Africa. Some German birds winter in gardens in the British Isles. Blackcaps eat insects when breeding, but otherwise survive primarily on small fruit; garden birds also eat bread, fat and
            <a href="/wiki/Peanut" title="Peanut">
             peanuts
            </a>
            in winter. The blackcap's range has been expanding, even though it is hunted in some countries. Its rich song has been featured in literature, films and music. (
            <b>
             <a href="/wiki/Eurasian_blackcap" title="Eurasian blackcap">
              Full article...
             </a>
            </b>
            )
           </p>

            <td style="padding:0 0.9em 0 0; width:300px;">
             <a class="image" href="/wiki/File:Paul_von_Hindenburg_(1914)_von_Nicola_Perscheid.jpg" title="Paul von Hindenburg">
              <img alt="Paul von Hindenburg" data-file-height="3855" data-file-width="2870" decoding="async" height="403" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Paul_von_Hindenburg_%281914%29_von_Nicola_Perscheid.jpg/300px-Paul_von_Hindenburg_%281914%29_von_Nicola_Perscheid.jpg" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Paul_von_Hindenburg_%281914%29_von_Nicola_Perscheid.jpg/450px-Paul_von_Hindenburg_%281914%29_von_Nicola_Perscheid.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Paul_von_Hindenburg_%281914%29_von_Nicola_Perscheid.jpg/600px-Paul_von_Hindenburg_%281914%29_von_Nicola_Perscheid.jpg 2x" width="300"/>
             </a>

            <td style="padding:0 6px 0 0">
             <p>
              <b>
               <a href="/wiki/Paul_von_Hindenburg" title="Paul von Hindenburg">
                Paul von Hindenburg
               </a>
              </b>
              (2 October 1847 – 2 August 1934), was a German general and statesman who led the
              <a href="/wiki/German_Army_(German_Empire)" title="German Army (German Empire)">
               Imperial German Army
              </a>
              during
              <a href="/wiki/World_War_I" title="World War I">
               World War I
              </a>
              . In 1925, he returned to public life to become the second elected president of the
              <a href="/wiki/Weimar_Republic" title="Weimar Republic">
               Weimar Republic
              </a>
              . While he was personally opposed to
              <a href="/wiki/Adolf_Hitler" title="Adolf Hitler">
               Adolf Hitler
              </a>
              and his
              <a href="/wiki/Nazi_Party" title="Nazi Party">
               Nazi Party
              </a>
              , he nonetheless played a major role in the political instability that resulted in their rise to power, ultimately agreeing to appoint Hitler as
              <a href="/wiki/Chancellor_of_Germany" title="Chancellor of Germany">
               Chancellor of Germany
              </a>
              in January 1933 after the Nazis had become the largest party in the
              <a href="/wiki/Reichstag_(Weimar_Republic)" title="Reichstag (Weimar Republic)">
               Reichstag
              </a>
              . This 1914 photograph of Hindenburg in military uniform was taken by the German photographer
              <a href="/wiki/Nicola_Perscheid" title="Nicola Perscheid">
               Nicola Perscheid
              </a>
              .
             </p>
